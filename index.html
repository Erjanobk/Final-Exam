City of Amsterdam

History of the city:

Amsterdam is the capital and the most popular city in the Netherlands. The beautiful city is located on the north side of the country and it's known as the Venice of the north due to its large number of canals. Amsterdam became one of the most important ports in the world in the 17th century and became the leading center of finance and trade. The city is also the cultural capital of the Netherlands. Many Dutch companies have their headquarters there such as Philips, AkzoNobel, TomTom, and ING. Also, many of the world's largest companies are based in Amsterdam or have established their European headquarters in the city, such as leading technology companies Uber, Netflix and Tesla.[18] In 2012, Amsterdam was ranked the second best city to live in. Amsterdam is one of the most popular cities for tourists, the city receives almost 5 million tourists annually. Also, the magnificent museums as Rijksmuseum ( Rijksmuseum) are the museum of the Netherlands. The completely renovated Rijksmuseum tells the story of the Netherlands from the Middle Ages to the 20th century.) Anne Frank House, Van Gogh Museum and many more.

Bos & Lommer

Bos en Lommer neighborhood got its name after a farm named Forest of Shade that used to exist on the same place where is today the main square of Bos en Lommer. It began in 1920 and 1930 among Admiraal de Ruijterkade, and consists mostly of apartment blocks and row housing. The place was improvised as a tent camp after WWII. The neighborhood is family orientated and a multicultural population, rents are cheaper than the center of the city so that also attracts students to more in. Jiu Jitsu

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is a martial art that focuses on grappling with particular emphasis on ground fighting. The sport was developed from Kodokan judo and was tought by the Japonese, eventually the sport came to be its own defined combat sport through the innovations, practices, and adaptation of judo. Jiu Jitsu is a self deffending sport that offers for smaller weaker people to be able to protect themselfes with ground fighting by joint locks and chokeholds.
